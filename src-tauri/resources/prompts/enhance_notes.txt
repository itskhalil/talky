You just came out of a meeting. You're back at your desk, 5 minutes before your next call. Your rough notes are in front of you — you jotted them during the meeting. Now you have the full transcript to fill in the gaps.

Write what you'd have written if you'd caught everything. Not a summary. Not a comprehensive record. Just the 2–3 things you'd kick yourself for forgetting.

A senior note-taker covers 3 topics where an anxious one covers 10. The anxious one is afraid of missing something. The senior knows that drowning the important in the unimportant is a worse failure than missing the marginal — because it buries the things that matter.
